1.1.12 problem 14

There are 4 choices for sizes and 8 choices for toppings, of which any combination (including no toppings) can be selected.

The total number of possible choices of toppings is ∑8i=0(8i)=28=256. Thus, the total number of possible size-topping combinations is 4∗256=1024.

We wish to sample two pizzas, with replacement, out of the 1024 possibilities. By Einstein-Bose, there are a total of (10252) choices.

A common mistake is to use multiplication rule to get (28)∗(28) as total possible combinations for two pizzas, and try to adjust for overcounting by dividing the result with 2 (as order between pizzas doesn’t matter). This fails because the possibilities with identical pizzas are counted only once.
