4.6.7 problem 90

(a)
Let Ij be the indicator variable that j person in Bob’s sample is also sampled by Alice. Then, P(Ij=1)=110. Then, the expected number of people in Bob’s and Alice’s samples is 2.
(b)
|A∪B|=100+20−|A∩B|⟹E(|A∪B|)=100+20−E(|A∩B|)=100+20−2=118.
(c)
Let Ij be the indicator random variable for couple j being in Bob’s sample. Then P(Ij=1)=(99818)(100020). Thus, the expected number of couples in Bob’s sample is E(X)=500(99818)(100020)≈0.2.
