2.3.4 problem 44

Let Gi be the event that the i-th door contains a goat, and let Di be the event that Monty opens door i. Let S be the event that the contestant is successful under his strategy.

(a)
There are two scenarios which result in the contestant selecting door 3 and Monty opening door 2. Either the car is behind door 3 and Monty randomly opens door 2, or doors 3 and 2 contain goats, and Monty opens door 2. Only the latter scenario results in a win for the contestant.

Thus,

P(S|D2,G2)=(p1+p2)p1p1+p2p312+(p1+p2)p1p1+p2=p1p1+12p3.

(b)
We can slightly modify the scenario in part a where doors 3 and 2 contain goats by multiplying the probability of the scenario by 12 to accomodate the chance that Monty might open the door with the car behind it.

P(S|D2,G2)=12(p1+p2)p1p1+p2p312+12(p1+p2)p1p1+p2p=12p112p1+12p3=p1p1+p3.

(c)
P(S|D2,G2)=p3p3+12p1.

(d)
P(S|D2,G2)=p3p3+p1.
