4.6.3 problem 86

(a)
P(X=x,Y=y,Z=z)=(nAx)(nBy)(nCz)(nm)
(b)
Let Ij be the indicator variable for person j in the sample being a member of party A. Then, X=∑mi=1Ii⟹E(X)=mnAn by symmetry.
(c)
Let’s find E(X2). If we square the expression for the sum of X’s constituent indicator r.v.s, we get

E(X2)=∑mi=1E(I2i)+2∗∑i<j,1≤j≤mE(IiIj)

Since I2i=Ii, we have ∑mi=1E(I2i)=m∗nAn

Additionally, for any pair i,j, the r.v. IiIj equals 1 only when some pair of samples are both members of party A, which occurs with probability nA(nA−1)n(n−1). There are (m2) pairs i,j. Therefore, the expression 2∗∑i<j,1≤j≤mE(IiIj) evaluates to nAm(m−1)(nA−1)n(n−1).

Finally, we have E(X2), so now we can write

Var(X)=E(X2)−EX2=m∗nAn+nAm(m−1)(nA−1)n(n−1)−m2n2An2

When m=1, Var(X)=nAn−(nAn)2=nAn(1−nAn)=nAn×nB+nCn.

When m=n, Var(X) = 0. This makes sense, as if the sample is the entire population, we always get the same number of members of party A in our sample (all of them), so there is no variation.
