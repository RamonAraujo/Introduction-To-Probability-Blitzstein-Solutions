3.1.1 problem 1

If the k-th person’s arrival results in the first birthday match, the first k−1 people have 365∗364∗⋯∗(365−k+2) choices of birthday assignments such that no two people have the same birthday. The k-th person has k−1 choices of birthdays, since their birthday must match that of one of the first k−1 people.

Thus,

P(X=k)=365∗364∗⋯∗(365−k+2)365k−1k−1365
