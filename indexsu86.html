2.6.3 problem 62

Let D be the event that the mother has the disease. Let Ci be the event that the i-th child has the disease.

(a)
P(Cc1∩Cc2)=P(D)P(Cc1∩Cc2|D)+P(Dc)P(Cc1∩Cc2|Dc)=13∗14+23=912
(b)
The two events are not independent. If the elder child has the disease, the mother has the disease, which means the younger child has probability 12 of having the disease. Unconditionally, the younger child has probability 16 of having the disease.
(c)
P(D|Cc1∩Cc2)=P(D)P(Cc1∩Cc2|D)P(Cc1∩Cc2)=13∗1413∗14+23=19
