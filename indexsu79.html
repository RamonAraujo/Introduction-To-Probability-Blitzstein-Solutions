2.4.2 problem 52

The problem is equivalent to betting 1 increments and having A start with ki dollars, while B starts with k(N−i) dollars.

Thus, p<12,

pi=1−(qp)ki1−(qp)kN.

Note that,

limk→∞1−(qp)ki1−(qp)kN=limk→∞−ki(qp)ki−1−kN(qp)kN−1=iNlimk→∞1(qp)k(N−i)=0.

This result makes sense, since p<12 implies that A should lose a game with high degree of certainty over the long run.
