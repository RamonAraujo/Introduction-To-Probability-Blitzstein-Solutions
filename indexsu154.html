4.2.5 problem 29

Random variable f(X) takes values that are the probabilities of a random value taken by X. Since X∼Geom(p), f(X)∈{(1−p)kp|k∈Z≥0}, and each value (1−p)kp of f(X) occurs with probability (1−p)kp. Thus,

E(X)=∞∑k=0((1−p)kp)2=−pp−2

for |p−1|2<1.
