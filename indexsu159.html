4.3.2 problem 39

Let Ij,1 and Ij,2 be the indicator random variables for the j-th person being sampled by the first and second researchers respectively.

P(Ij,1=1)=(N−1m−1)(Nm). P(Ij,2=1)=(N−1n−1)(Nn). Since sampling is done independently by the two researchers, P(Ij,1=1,Ij,2=1)=(N−1m−1)(N−1n−1)(Nm)(Nn).

Let X=∑nj=1(Ij,1Ij,2) be the number of people sampled by both researchers. Then,
