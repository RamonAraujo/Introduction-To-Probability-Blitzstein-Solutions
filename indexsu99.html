3.1.2 problem 2

(a)
Since the trials are independent, the probability that the first k−1 trials fail is (12)k−1, and the probability that the k-th trial is successful is 12. Thus, for k≥1,

P(X=k)=(12)k−1∗12.

(b)
This problem reduces to part a once a trial is performed. Whatever it’s outcome, we label it failure and proceed to perform more trials until the opposite outcome is observed. Thus, for k≥2,

P(X=k)=(12)k−2∗12.
