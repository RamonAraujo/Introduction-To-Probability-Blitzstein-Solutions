3.1.4 problem 4

To show that F(x) is a CDF, we need to show that F is increasing, right-continuous, and converges to 0 and 1 in the limits.

The first condition is true since ⌊x⌋ is increasing.

Since limx→a+F(x)=F(a) when a∈N by the definition of F(x), the second condition is satisfied.

limx→∞F(x)=1 by the definition of F(x), and also, by definition, limx→−∞F(x)=0. Thus, the third condition is satisfied, and F(x) is a CDF.

The PMF F corresponds to is

P(X=k)=1n

for 1≤k≤n and 0 everywhere else.
